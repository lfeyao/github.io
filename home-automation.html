Home Automation

Posted on Mon 04 June 2018 in Coding

As a new parent, you'll find your kids want to watch the same videos/listen to the same songs over and over and over. For us, this has jumped between Moana, Frozen, Baby Shark, Lion King, StoryBots and Baby Einstein.

Being able to play these videos/songs on demand via voice has been a huge help. Below is what I used to make my life easier.

Hardware:

  • Amazon Dot - Very beneficial because of the Amazon music library included with Amazon Prime. I use Dot which has a 3.5mm jack to connect with my receiver. Has a number of other Alexa skills you can enable via app. Can also link to IFTTT via trigger command (I've used this to track babies feeding/poop etc).

  • Google Mini - Mainly useful right now because of ability to directly say which youtube video you want to play (Alexa can't do this)

  • Logitech Hub - Relays set of commands to your TV, receiver, etc like a universal remote except works with your phone via app and also with Alexa/Google.

Software:

  • https://github.com/rg3/youtube-dl - Downloads youtube videos to your computer. Useful for skipping ads. Also if your kids like a particular song unavailable on Amazon music, you can extract audio to mp3 and upload to your music library on Amazon to use with Alexa (youtube-dl -x --audio-format mp3 [link])

  • https://github.com/xat/castnow - You can cast any home videos in mp4 and downloaded youtube videos to your chromecast.

  • (Optional) https://github.com/sushilks/harmonyHubCLI - Command line interface for your Harmony Hub. Useful for really lazy people that want to bundle 3 voice commands into one bash command.

  • (Optional) Termius for IOS - Allows you to ssh into your server from your phone and issue commands from your phone.

That's it, hopefully this was useful for some new parents!
